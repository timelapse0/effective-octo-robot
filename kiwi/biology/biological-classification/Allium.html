Wild Leek is a large genus of about 400 species. They are biennial blubs in the onion family native to Southern Europe, Asia, and Macronesia but are found all over the world. The genus is divided into three different groups:

  • Porrum Group is leaks grown for the edible stems. 
  • Ampeloprassum Group is elephant garlic and levant garlic.
  • Kurrat Group is also known as Egyptian Leek or Salad Leek but grown mainly for the leaves.
